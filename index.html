Robert Metcalfe

The man who co-invented the ethernet cable and pioneered the beginning of the internet

Here's a time line of Robert Metcalfelife:

  • 1946 - Born in Brooklyn New York
  • 1969 - Graduates from MIT with a B.S. Electrical Engineering, B.S. Industrial Management,"
  • 1973 - Graduates from Harvard with a Ph.D in Computer Science"
  • 1973 - Metcalfe was working at PARC in 1973 when he and David Boggs invented Ethernet, initially a standard for connecting computers over short distances. Metcalfe identifies the day Ethernet was born as May 22, 1973
  • 1979 - Metcalfe departed PARC and co-founded 3Com
  • 1990 -n 1990, in a boardroom skirmish,[7] the 3Com board of directors chose Éric Benhamou to succeed Bill Krause as CEO of the networking company instead of Metcalfe.Metcalfe left 3Com and began a 10-year stint as a publisher and pundit, writing an Internet column for InfoWorld
  • 2010 - In November 2010 Metcalfe was selected to lead innovation initiatives at The University of Texas at Austin's Cockrell School of Engineering
  • 2021 - He began his appointment in January 2011 and retired as an Emeritus Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the end of 2021
"Without the work of Robert and David we wouldn't have the internet ."
